# Google Play Store payment

> This article is provided by **CardNav [cardnav.xyz](https://cardnav.xyz)**.

If you do not have an Apple device, using an Android phone and Google Play is also an alternative for avoiding direct card-binding rejection.

The specific practical steps are as follows:

- **Prepare network and account**: Enable a network node in the corresponding overseas target region (see [VPN and proxy access](./321-tool-vpn.md)), and log in to or register a brand-new overseas-region Google account on an Android phone.
- **Associate the app and download**: Uninstall the original version, log in to your overseas account in the Google Play Store, and re-download the official large model App client.
- **Bind an international payment card**: In the payment profile of Google Play's personal center, add and bind your dual-currency foreign-currency credit card, such as Visa / Mastercard.
- **The app triggers in-app purchase payment**: Open the large model App and click subscribe, then choose the bound foreign-currency card in the Google Play payment popup that is invoked to complete the transaction.
